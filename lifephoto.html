Bygge Hygge: Deconstructing Happiness in Denmark

As a Glavin Global Fellowship Grantee, in May 2022, I had the opportunity to visit one of the genuinely happiest places on Earth: Billund, Denmark, home of LEGO. During my five-day research journey, I worked to deconstruct LEGO’s “bygge hygge,” that is, the culture of “creating coziness” – the secret sauce of establishing happiness. I had the chance to tour the world headquarters of LEGO Group, meet with several LEGO associates, and build some of my own LEGO creations. I also traveled to Copenhagen to fully immerse myself in Danish culture. While in the capital, I interacted with dozens of locals who provided insights on what makes the country so unique. The most common reason: the country’s social welfare system which includes free education and health care, livable monthly support income during periods of unemployment, etc. Additionally, I visited museums, toured the Happiness Research Institute, and I rode a bicycle around the city to experience as much of it as I could and get to my next meals more quickly! One unexpected surprise was an invitation to join a group of Danish school children as they tried their hand at dirt bike jumping. As someone whose mission is to change the world for the better, I feel privileged to have brought a little bit of Babson to Denmark, been changed myself as a result of the journey, and cannot wait to continue to explore the world.
